Planetary Hours

Planetary hours are a traditional system of sacred timekeeping. Each day is ruled by one of the seven classical planets. The hours themselves are governed in sequence by the Chaldean order.

The day begins at sunrise, not midnight. The first hour after sunrise is ruled by the planet of the day. Each hour that follows proceeds in the ancient planetary order.

Daytime and nighttime hours are unequal. Each is divided into twelve parts based on the actual motion of the Sun. This makes planetary hours living and variable.

These hours have been used for ritual work, contemplation, study, and timing for over two thousand years.

This application calculates planetary hours using real solar motion and astronomical planetary positions.
